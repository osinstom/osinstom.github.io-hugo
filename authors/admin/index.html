My name is Tomek Osiński and I’m the author of this blog. The blog is full of networking topics, which I find interesting and worth to share with community. Mainly, I am going to publish tech tutorials & reports. However, some of my personal opinions or visions may also appear on this blog. Note that the posts are on my own and don’t represent the opinions of my employer.

I’m a R&D Software Engineer in the area of Software-Defined Networking & Network Functions Virtualization and also an Open-Source enthusiast. My R&D activities are related to network softwarization including network automation & orchestration, Software-Defined Infrastructure (i.a. SDN), Network Function Virtualization (NFV), Network Slicing, mobile networks and Telco Cloud technologies. Currently, my research is focused on improving the performance of the NFV workload, therefore I deal with data plane programmability and packet processing techniques for SDN and NFV systems. I’m involved in the project related to the P4 technology and OpenStack. So far, I have been contributing to various open-source projects including Floodlight, ONOS and ONAP.

Professionally, I work as a R&D Expert at Orange Labs Poland. Moreover, I am a PhD student at Warsaw University of Technology and the member of SDN and Network Virtualization Lab.
